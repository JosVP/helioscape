Create a plan for stylized flat day/night lighting and camera framing in the orrery for Helioscape.

CONTEXT
The orrery currently uses a dim AmbientLight (~0.05) + a strong DirectionalLight (~2.0), giving a realistic but muddy look, and the camera leaves ~30% dead space at the top and ~5% at the bottom with the sun off to one side. We want a STYLIZED, FLAT day/night look (Kurzgesgant-inspired, our own spin) where each planet's sun-facing side is evenly lit, the far side is flat and dark, and a thin bright rim separates them. And we want the sun better centred using the wasted vertical space.

Read AGENTS.md and docs/agents/ARCHITECTURE.md (Three.js, Canvas components, Visual value pattern). Owned by:
- src/app/features/orrery/orrery-scene.builder.ts
- src/app/features/orrery/orrery.component.ts

REQUIREMENTS

1. Orbit-based day/night shading (flat, not realistic)
   - For each planet, the lit hemisphere is the one facing the sun (origin). Compute the sun direction per planet from its current orbital position (sun at origin → planet position vector). This is pure and derived from position — no stored animation state (Visual value pattern).
   - Lit side: flat, even illumination of the planet's texture (no strong specular, no falloff gradient across the disc — keep it graphic and even). Dark side: the texture rendered flat and dark (toward the night-side variant where one exists, e.g. Earth city lights from 18-2).
   - Between lit and dark: a THIN bright highlight rim (configurable width, default ~3–5% of the disc) along the terminator — a signature stylised edge, not a soft gradient.
   - Implement with whichever approach is cleanest and disposable: a custom ShaderMaterial with a sun-direction uniform updated per frame is the natural fit (flat term = step/smoothstep on dot(normal, sunDir) with a narrow highlight band). If a shader is used, keep uniforms minimal and update them in the RAF loop from locals.

2. Configurability
   - Expose: highlight rim width, lit-side brightness, dark-side brightness/tint, and a master toggle, as builder/material parameters with documented defaults. The user wants to tune this without editing shader internals.

3. Lighting rig cleanup
   - Replace or retune the AmbientLight/DirectionalLight so the new flat shading dominates and planets are not double-lit. If the shader fully controls planet appearance, reduce scene lights to whatever the sun glow/other meshes still need. Keep it intentional and documented.

4. Camera framing — use the dead space, centre the sun
   - Re-frame so the sun sits closer to the visual centre and the ~30% top / ~5% bottom dead space is used (orbits fill the frame more symmetrically). Adjust camera position/target and/or fov in the builder. Keep the existing "camera does not move except planet zoom" rule — this is a one-time framing change, not controls.
   - Verify all four orbits remain fully visible at the default canvas aspect, and that the framing still works when the canvas resizes (respect the existing resize handling).

RAF DISCIPLINE (non-negotiable)
Read signals once into locals at the top of animate(). Update the sun-direction uniform(s) from planet positions already computed that frame. No allocation inside the loop. Per ARCHITECTURE.md never call signal getters mid-render.

CLEANUP
Any new ShaderMaterial / uniforms / lights MUST be disposed in the existing ngOnDestroy traversal.

OUT OF SCOPE (later blocks): pixelate post-process + sun/atmosphere glow (18-4), orbit-ring interaction + removing the planet gray-out (18-5), planet texture generation (18-2, dependency — assume textures incl. night-side variants exist).
