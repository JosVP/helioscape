## DLC — THREE SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Post-V1 expansion. Three star systems, distinct identity per system. Base game engine/tech mostly reuses — DLC = new content + few new gameplay systems. Time moves faster in DLC: "time is an investment" arc already established. Culture events sparser + more concentrated. Player arrives with full tech + knowledge.

Communication lag between systems = feature: each system runs semi-independently, transmissions arrive on lag timescales. Player makes decisions for one, sends instructions to another, waits for results from third. Natural rhythm of attention — no system demands constant focus.

**Narrative arc:** Binary = applying + refining knowledge under new constraints. BHS = experimenting with what life/civilisation can become. Archive = looking back at what we were. Beginning → becoming → remembering.

## DLC 1 — BINARY STAR SYSTEM (The Challenge System)

Player applies full base-game knowledge under different physical constraints. Variable irradiance: both stars same side = hotter; opposite sides = more even heating but complex day/night cycle. Terraforming timelines shift based on stellar orbital config. Habitable zone narrower + less stable. Player discovers base-game assumptions don't quite hold.

**Twist world:** one planet already partially terraformed by natural processes — not humans. Challenge = managing world that is *almost* right rather than transforming from scratch. Different skill, same systems.

Goal: terraform + colonise new planets. Familiar loop, harder constraints.

## DLC 2 — BLACK HOLE SYSTEM / BHS (The Alien System)

Stellar-mass black hole (~10 solar masses) fed continuously with star-lifted or redirected matter. **Accretion disk** = light + heat source (not Hawking radiation — negligible at this scale; not Penrose process — no visible output). Disk actively fed → requires ongoing matter input to maintain luminosity. Stop feeding → dims over decades. BHS ongoing management layer — unlike a star, not self-sustaining.

### What you see from surface
Not clean solar disk. Gravitationally-lensed accretion ring: bright on side moving toward you (Doppler boosting), dimmer on retreating side, dark centre (event horizon shadow). Back of disk wraps around and is visible simultaneously with front. First-gen colonists find this deeply unsettling. Children born there find it normal. That generational shift = CE series.

Light: blue-white to yellow-white depending on feeding rate. Harder, bluer spectrum than Sol. Vitamin D synthesis needs supplementation or atmospheric UV filtering. Circadian rhythms manageable with artificial lighting supplements. Shadows soft + directionally complex — accretion ring subtends significant angle, producing multiple partial shadows. Artists on this world develop entirely different techniques for representing light.

### Orbital distances and gravity
At 3-4 AU from 10-solar-mass black hole, gravitational pull comparable to Earth's solar experience. Tidal forces negligible at this distance — danger zone within ~0.1 AU. Planets structurally safe at habitable distances. Same orrery display, different central object.

### The Black Forest ecosystem
Under blue/UV-dominant spectrum, green is inefficient pigment. Plants maximise absorption from violet/blue:
- **Colouration:** jet black or deep purple foliage
- **UV protection:** metallic or iridescent sheens (beetle-wing structure) reflecting excess UV
- **Growth rhythm:** pulsed cycles driven by accretion disk's natural flickering (variable luminosity minutes to hours based on feeding rate variability) rather than stable day/night cycle

Insects: accretion disk light heavily polarised by intense magnetic fields. Insects evolve super-navigation via polarisation — millimetre-precision location awareness from sky. Flowers evolve intricate UV patterns (neon landing strips to insects, plain black blobs to unaugmented humans).

Animals: if disk light too steady, circadian rhythms weaken. Animals may evolve rhythms based on disk flickering — biological clock based on luminosity pulses, not 24-hour cycle.

**Soundscape:** organisms evolved under variable EM radiation + strong magnetic fields communicate more heavily through sound + vibration than colour. EM signals less reliable carriers in this environment. BHS fauna extraordinarily vocal — complex songs, echolocation, seismic ground communication. First CE from surface notes the night side is deafeningly loud.

**X-ray shielding = mandatory infrastructure.** Accretion disks are significant X-ray sources. Strong planetary magnetosphere not optional — existential. Magnetic shield failure threatens entire pollinator population → food chain collapse. BHS equivalent of L1 magnetic umbrella — permanent, critical, maintained via infrastructure health system.

### BHS Planet 1 — Human Habitat
Standard habitable world under alien light. Design principle: don't fight BHS environment, design with it. Black-purple vegetation, UV-pattern gardens, architecture oriented around accretion disk position rather than compass rose. CEs: what does home mean when home looks nothing like Earth? Third-gen colonists who have never seen a yellow star. Their art. Their mythology about the black hole they call their sun.

### BHS Planet 2 — Fauna and Flora Only (No Permanent Human Presence)
Seeded using biological composition interface. Humans withdraw after ecosystem stabilises. Orbital observation continues — player watches world change from space, sees species ranges shifting, unexpected developments emerge. May intervene or not. World evolves beyond what was designed.

First CE after withdrawal: something unexpected emerged within fifty years. Not threatening — just surprising. Life doing something nobody predicted. Honest about limits of biological composition interface: designed starting conditions, not outcome.

This world = ethical question made physical: we engineered entire biosphere then left. Is it ours? Is it theirs?

### BHS Planet 3 — Extreme Seasonal Variability (Elliptical Orbit)
Elliptical orbit. Periapsis = intense accretion disk radiation, brief. Apoapsis = significantly reduced radiation, prolonged. Planet rushes through bright season quickly, spends more time in dim cool part of orbit.

Ecosystem adaptations: rapid-growth strategies during bright season, deep dormancy during long dim period. Mass simultaneous flowering + reproduction across entire ecosystem during bright season — everything at once because energy window is brief. Dim season: quiet, slow, only cold-metabolism organisms visibly active, disk a faint smear.

Vignette contrast between seasons = most dramatic in entire DLC: same location, completely different worlds.

## DLC 3 — ARCHIVE SYSTEM (The Emotional Payoff)

No terraforming for humanity's benefit. No colonisation goals. Retrospective — looking back at what Earth was, what was lost, what deserves to be remembered. Emotionally opposite of Binary System's challenge loop.

### Archive Planet 1 — The Sanctuary
De-extincted species given an entire world. Earth-evolved organisms belong here, not in alien BHS environment.

Player chooses de-extinction category at founding (or all three on separate continents — temporal layers that never naturally coexisted):

- **Recent losses (~last 500 years):** dodo, passenger pigeon, thylacine, Steller's sea cow, great auk. Directly human-caused. Good DNA from museum specimens.
- **Megafauna losses (10,000–50,000 years ago):** woolly mammoth, cave lion, Irish elk, woolly rhinoceros, short-faced bear, giant ground sloth. Ancient DNA exists but degraded — requires gap-filling.
- **Deep time (millions–hundreds of millions of years ago):** non-avian dinosaurs, Megalodon, Cambrian organisms. No usable DNA — reconstruction from fossil morphology + evolutionary modelling. What arrives = approximation. CE is honest: *"We called them dinosaurs. Whether they would have recognised themselves by that name is a question we cannot answer. We built our best understanding of what they were, gave it a heartbeat, and let it go. The rest is theirs."*

Dinosaur world = most memorable moment in entire game including base game. Not gameplay — what it means. Civilisation that can terraform planets + build Dyson swarms deciding to spend that power on bringing back Tyrannosaurus rex is a statement about what we value. CE when first dinosaurs confirmed self-sustaining should be very short. Just the fact of it.

### Archive Planet 2 — The Ocean World (Europa / Enceladus Life)
If Europa life discovered in base game: organisms given entire ocean planet with access to accretion disk or stellar light — energy source they have never had in billions of years of subsurface evolution. Chemotrophs that evolved in cold dark hydrothermal environments have never experienced photosynthesis. Sun-lit ocean changes energy budget completely. What they become = unknown. Withdrawal after seeding, observation only.

If Europa crashed for Venus Path C: player uses Enceladus life instead — or world becomes speculative seeding using engineered approximations of what subsurface ocean life probably looked like. Different emotional weight depending on base game playthrough.

### Archive Planet 3 — The Archive World
Entire planet as physical record of humanity's history, built for audience that does not yet exist. Not museum for humans — message to whatever intelligence exists in this universe in five billion years.

Physically: mountain ranges carved into geometric patterns encoding binary data visible from orbit. Mineral veins arranged to preserve chemical records of Earth's biosphere. Monumental structures built to survive geological timescales, not human ones. Voyager golden record scaled to a planet.

Gameplay: player selects what to encode — categories drawn from actual playthrough. Which Mars path? Encoded in geology. Which CEs flagged in history book? Carved into cliff faces in language designed to be decodable by any sufficiently advanced intelligence. Europa decision, whatever it was, is here. First rain on Mars is here. Red Season is here.

No mechanics after designation. No management. Planet simply exists, permanent, carrying its record forward.

**Closing image of full game including all DLC:** Archive World from orbit, at night. Geometric mountain ranges visible from space, encoding everything. No humans present. Cut to black. No score. No summary. Just that.

## DLC SCOPE NOTE
Three systems ambitious but justified: most base game engine + systems reuse directly. New content: new planet visuals + vignettes, new CEs, BHS-specific ecosystem mechanics (X-ray shielding as mandatory infra, accretion disk feeding management), elliptical orbit seasonal system for BHS Planet 3, de-extinction category selection for Sanctuary, archive encoding selection for Archive World. Binary System reuses almost all existing mechanics with modified parameters.

Release as separate DLC packs, not one bundle: Binary System first (most mechanically familiar, lowest production cost) → BHS second (highest visual + content investment) → Archive System third (lowest gameplay complexity, highest emotional payoff — right note to end on).
